### Model Releases and Updates
- **Kimi K2.5 by Moonshot AI**: A new open-source multimodal model released, claimed to outperform proprietary models like Gemini 3, Opus 4.5, or GPT-5.2 in benchmarks. It's designed for broad applications without task-specific fine-tuning.
- **Hermes 4 70B**: An open-source reasoning model that beats Grok 4 and Gemini 2.5 in tests for coding, logic, and writing. It's free to use and positioned as the top open-source reasoning model for 2025.
- **Indonesia Lifts Ban on Grok**: The country conditionally lifted its ban on xAI's Grok model, allowing access after addressing regulatory concerns. This expands Grok's availability in Southeast Asia.

### New Research Papers
- **Scalable Power Sampling: Unlocking Efficient Training-Free Inference**: Introduces a method to make LLMs double-check their work without retraining, improving accuracy like a student verifying calculations.
- **Self-Distillation Enables Continual Adaptation in Large Language Models**: Proposes Self-Distillation Fine-Tuning (SDFT) from MIT and ETH Zurich, allowing LLMs to learn new skills from demonstrations without catastrophic forgetting.
- **Mechanistic Data Attribution: Tracing the Training Origins of Interpretable LLM Units**: Explores how to trace LLM behaviors back to specific training data, aiding interpretability in models.
- **ChatGPT for Medical Information Extraction: Performance, Explainability, and Beyond**: Evaluates ChatGPT's use in extracting medical data, with a deep dive into performance and explainability.
- **Authority Signals in AI Health Sources: Evaluating Credibility in ChatGPT Answers**: Assesses how ChatGPT evaluates source credibility in health-related responses.
- **AI Swarms Could Invade Social Media, Threaten Democracy**: A Science Journal study warns that AI bot swarms could spread misinformation and harass users, mimicking human behavior to undermine democracy.
- Multiple new arXiv submissions (Jan 30, 2026): Over 275 papers in AI, including topics like reasoning reward models for agents and adaptive subnetworks for heterogeneous data. Key examples include "Exploring Reasoning Reward Model for Agents" (arXiv:2601.22154) and "Routing the Lottery: Adaptive Subnetworks for Heterogeneous Data" (arXiv:2601.22141).

### Open-Source Projects and Tools
- **OpenClaw (formerly Clawdbot/Moltbot)**: A rebranded open-source project for personal AI assistants, emphasizing security, community-driven development, and self-hosting after recent chaos with scams and hijackings.
- **OpenAI Prism**: A free tool for scientists to generate research papers, handling hypothesis, data analysis, and LaTeX formatting. Raises concerns about flooding journals with AI-generated content.
- **Project Genie by Google DeepMind**: An experimental prototype for Google AI Ultra users, allowing creation and exploration of interactive worlds via text prompts and images. Powered by Genie 3 for real-time generation.
